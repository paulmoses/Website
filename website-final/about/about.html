About the show

Romeo & Juliet is the West-End’s new hip-hop dance performance based on the classic love tragedy by William Shakespeare. Set in the South Bronx in the year of 1988, the show gives a pure and honest reflection of the culture. The music genre of hip-hop was on the up but unfortunately so was crime and poverty in the area. Romeo is a member of the Black Spades gang whereas Juliet is the cousin of Tybalt, a well-respected member of the Savage Nomads gang. They are both poor and homeless, but have a passion for hip-hop music. The edgy performance in London’s Picadilly Theatre features music from some of the most iconic names in old school hip-hop including Run DMC, Public Enemy, Eric B & Rankin, De La Soul, EPMD and A Tribe Called Quest.

Half the profits made from ticket sales will be donated to Coalition for the Homeless, a charity aiming to end homelessness in New York City. Romeo & Juliet will be only be on for 2 nights in January, so make sure to get your tickets NOW!
